# OpenAI Build Week Submission — Clarity Ledger

## One-line description

An AI evidence and contradiction workbench that traces operational guidance to its source, flags conflicts, and routes decision-ready clarification before automation acts.

## Category

Work & Productivity

## Description

Teams rarely suffer from a complete absence of information. They suffer from too many versions of it: an approved policy, a later email, an onboarding checklist, a finance memo, and informal chat guidance that appear individually reasonable but disagree in practice.

Clarity Ledger turns that fragmented guidance into an evidence-preserving workflow. It registers each source with its date, owner, authority, and cited excerpt; surfaces contradiction candidates side by side; explains why each conflict needs review; drafts a focused clarification question; and maps which follow-on workflows are safe to automate with human approval.

The Build Week demo uses a fictional organization and synthetic evidence. It demonstrates three realistic conflict patterns—remote-work approval, expense receipt deadlines, and incident-reporting timelines—without using private or regulated material.

Unlike a chatbot that simply chooses an answer, Clarity Ledger makes uncertainty visible. The system is designed to assist investigation and routing, not establish policy, enforce compliance, or make personnel decisions.

## Under-three-minute demo script

**0:00–0:20 — Problem**  
“Operational guidance lives in many places, and search can return two plausible answers without telling you they conflict. Clarity Ledger makes every instruction traceable and every conflict reviewable.”

**0:20–0:50 — Source register**  
“This synthetic workspace contains six sources across policy, email, checklist, memo, handbook, and chat. Each record preserves its date, owner, authority, status, and exact cited excerpt.”

**0:50–1:25 — Analysis**  
“I’ll run evidence analysis. The workbench finds three contradiction candidates and opens the highest-value review view. Here, an approved remote-work policy conflicts with a later informal supervisor email. Clarity Ledger does not guess which one wins.”

**1:25–1:55 — Clarification**  
“Instead, it explains the discrepancy and creates a decision-ready question tied to both source IDs. The clarification queue shows the responsible owners and the evidence trail needed to resolve it.”

**1:55–2:20 — Safe automation**  
“The automation map separates low-risk assistance, such as source intake, from high-risk actions, such as propagating policy changes. Every consequential step retains human approval.”

**2:20–2:45 — Build process**  
“I used Codex as the implementation environment and GPT-5.6 for the reasoning-intensive evidence model, contradiction explanations, clarification workflow, and safety boundaries. This demo is deterministic and synthetic so it works without credentials and exposes no private data.”

**2:45–2:55 — Close**  
“Clarity Ledger: every instruction has a source, and every conflict has a trail.”
